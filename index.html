Recognize — تعرّف

Warning signs to watch for

Someone you love may be struggling in silence. These signs can help you see what words often cannot say.

🌑

Withdrawal from others

Pulling away from family, friends, prayer circles — avoiding the people and places they once loved.

💬

Talking about death

Saying things like "I wish I wasn't here," "everyone would be better off without me," or giving away prized possessions.

🪞

Hopelessness

A deep belief that nothing will ever get better — that they are beyond help, beyond mercy, beyond hope.

⚡

Sudden calmness

After a period of depression, a sudden sense of peace can sometimes mean a decision has been made. Take it seriously.

🕌

Loss of faith connection

Stopping prayers, abandoning the Quran, feeling that Allah has abandoned them. Pain can disconnect people from their anchor.

😶

Emotional numbness

Feeling nothing at all — no joy, no sadness, no connection. A blankness that is its own kind of suffering.

Act — تحرّك

How to help someone

🚨 In immediate danger? Act now.

If someone is in immediate danger, do not leave them alone. Call for emergency help immediately and stay with them until help arrives.

🇲🇦 Morocco: 141
Emergency: 15
Police: 19
١

Listen without judgment

Don't rush to fix or advise. Sit with them. Say: "I'm here. I hear you. You don't have to face this alone." Presence is often more powerful than words.

٢

Ask directly

Asking "Are you thinking about suicide?" does not plant the idea — it opens a door. Asking shows you care enough to go there with them.

٣

Remove means of harm

Quietly and without drama, put distance between them and anything that could cause harm. This small act can save a life.

٤

Connect them to professional help

A mental health professional is not a sign of weakness — it is wisdom. Help them take that first step. Offer to go with them.

٥

Stay. Follow up. Keep showing up.

Recovery is not a single moment. Keep checking in. A message, a call, a visit. Let them know they have not been forgotten.

Faith — إيمان

What Islam teaches us

إِنَّ مَعَ الْعُسْرِ يُسْرًا

"With hardship comes ease."
Surah Ash-Sharh, 94:6 — Allah repeats this twice in the same surah. The ease is already promised.

لَا تَقْنَطُوا مِن رَّحْمَةِ اللَّهِ

"Do not despair of the mercy of Allah."
Surah Az-Zumar, 39:53 — No darkness is deeper than Allah's light. No sin is greater than His forgiveness.

وَهُوَ مَعَكُمْ أَيْنَ مَا كُنتُمْ

"He is with you wherever you are."
Surah Al-Hadid, 57:4 — Even in your loneliest, darkest 3am — you are not unseen.

✦ ✦ ✦

Your life is not yours to take

Allah created you with purpose. Not by accident, not as an afterthought — but deliberately, with a specific role that only you can fill in this world. Your pain is real. Your exhaustion is real. But so is the mercy of the One who sees every tear that falls in the dark.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "No fatigue, nor disease, nor sorrow, nor sadness, nor hurt, nor distress befalls a Muslim — even if it were the prick he receives from a thorn — but that Allah expiates some of his sins." Your suffering is not wasted. It is seen, counted, and honoured.

Stay. The morning you are afraid will never come — it will come. The ease that feels impossible — it is already written. The people who need your presence — they exist, even if you haven't met them yet. You matter. Your soul matters. Your story is not finished.

"Be in this world as though you are a stranger or a wayfarer."
— Hadith, Ibn Umar (r.a.), Sahih al-Bukhari
